# 07 — Business Insights

Insights below are derived directly from the underlying model data (full-year totals, no filters applied).

## By Location

| Location | Total Sales | Total Revenue | Total Calls Handled |
|---|---|---|---|
| Delhi | 11,301 | 206,901.56 | 131,469 |
| Kolkatta | 9,805 | 206,154.52 | 125,973 |
| Mumbai | 9,718 | 185,506.96 | 119,432 |
| Bangalore | 7,649 | 147,620.44 | 102,517 |

- **Delhi and Kolkatta are effectively tied on revenue** (~207K vs ~206K) despite Delhi having ~1,500 more units sold — Kolkatta is generating more revenue per unit sold.
- **Bangalore consistently trails** across all three metrics — lowest sales, revenue, and call volume — making it a candidate for deeper investigation (market size, product mix, or marketing coverage).

## By Product — Volume vs Revenue Diverge

| Product | Total Sales (units) | Total Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| Smartphone | 5,422 | 119,717.76 |
| XBox | 5,419 | 94,724.12 |
| Tablets | 5,152 | 61,617.92 |
| Desktops | 5,056 | 106,984.96 |
| Laptops | 4,784 | 61,617.92 |
| Ipods | 4,517 | 116,357.92 |
| Camera | 4,383 | 112,906.08 |
| Ipad | 3,740 | 72,256.80 |

- **Smartphone is the clear leader on both volume and revenue** — the strongest overall performer.
- **Tablets and Laptops rank high on units sold (3rd and 5th) but lowest on revenue** — both generate the same total revenue (61,617.92) despite different volumes, indicating a low price point per unit relative to other categories.
- **Camera and Ipods rank mid-to-low on units sold but punch well above their weight on revenue** (3rd and 2nd highest revenue respectively) — a strong signal of high price-per-unit / high-margin products worth protecting or promoting further.

## By Quarter

| Quarter | Total Sales | Total Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 | 10,461 | 202,100.08 |
| Q3 | 9,740 | 186,730.56 |
| Q4 | 9,379 | 181,881.24 |
| Q2 | 8,893 | 175,471.60 |

- **Q1 is the strongest quarter** on both sales and revenue, roughly 8-15% ahead of the other three quarters.
- **Q2 is consistently the weakest quarter** — worth checking whether this reflects a seasonal demand dip or a specific operational issue (e.g. inventory, staffing) in April–June.

## Sales vs Call-Center Load

- Locations with higher sales and revenue (Delhi, Kolkatta) also carry the highest call volumes — consistent with call volume scaling alongside commercial activity rather than indicating a support-efficiency problem.
- Because revenue-per-unit varies significantly by product (see above), **raw call volume by product is not a reliable proxy for revenue importance** — Camera and Ipods deserve support-resourcing attention proportional to their revenue contribution, not just their transaction count.
