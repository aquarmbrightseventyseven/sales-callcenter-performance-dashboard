# 08 — Recommendations

Based on the insights in [07_Business_Insights.md](07_Business_Insights.md), the following actions are recommended:

## Commercial

1. **Investigate Bangalore's underperformance.** It trails every other location on sales, revenue, and call volume. Determine whether this reflects smaller market size, weaker local marketing/distribution, or a product-mix mismatch, and pilot a targeted push (promotions, expanded product availability) to close the gap.
2. **Protect and grow high-margin products.** Camera and Ipods generate outsized revenue relative to their unit sales. Consider increasing marketing spend or shelf/placement priority for these categories rather than over-indexing on high-volume, low-revenue items like Tablets and Laptops.
3. **Understand the Q2 dip.** Q2 is the weakest quarter on both sales and revenue across the year. If this is seasonal, plan promotions or bundle offers in April–June to smooth the curve; if operational (stock, staffing), address the root cause ahead of the next cycle.
4. **Study the Delhi/Kolkatta revenue parity.** Kolkatta earns nearly the same revenue as Delhi with ~13% fewer units sold, suggesting a more favorable product mix or pricing there — worth replicating in other locations.

## Operational (Call Center)

5. **Resource support by revenue contribution, not just call volume.** Since call volume tracks sales volume rather than revenue, high-revenue/lower-volume products (Camera, Ipods) may be under-resourced relative to their business importance. Consider revenue-weighted staffing or SLA prioritization.
6. **Benchmark handle-time efficiency by location.** Delhi has both the highest call volume and highest total handle time — confirm this scales proportionally rather than reflecting a per-call efficiency gap versus other locations.
